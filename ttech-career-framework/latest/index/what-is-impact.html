What is Impact?

Overview

At TTech, we believe that impact is the most important measure of success. Impact isn’t just about what you do—it’s about the value you create for our company, our customers, and our team members. This guide helps you understand how we think about and measure impact across all engineering roles.

Core Principles of Impact

Consistency

We consider the impact you’ve had over time. Are you able to deliver work that matters not just once, but do so sustainably? Consistency means:

  • Maintaining high quality in your deliverables

  • Regularly meeting or exceeding expectations

  • Building sustainable solutions that last

  • Being a reliable team member

  • Consistently contributing to team success

Velocity

We consider the rate at which you deliver impact, not just the cumulative impact that you have achieved. Velocity encompasses:

  • Efficient delivery of solutions

  • Smart prioritization of work

  • Reducing unnecessary complexity

  • Making well-reasoned technical decisions

  • Helping others move faster

Accountability

Every level is accountable for delivering impact. More senior levels are held more directly accountable for the business impact of their decisions. This means:

  • Taking full ownership of outcomes and their long-term implications

  • Making and standing behind decisions with clear rationale

  • Learning from failures and taking responsibility for improvements

  • Being proactive about identifying and solving problems

  • Considering long-term implications of technical decisions

  • Ensuring knowledge transfer and documentation for sustainability

  • Taking initiative to improve processes and systems

  • Following through on commitments and deliverables

Ownership

Ownership is a fundamental principle at TTech. It means:

  • Taking full responsibility for the success of your work

  • Proactively identifying and addressing potential issues

  • Ensuring long-term sustainability of solutions

  • Taking initiative to improve existing systems

  • Following through on commitments

  • Maintaining clear communication about progress and challenges

  • Ensuring proper documentation and knowledge transfer

  • Supporting team members and contributing to team success

How We Measure Impact

Technical Impact

  • Quality of code and systems

  • Technical innovation

  • System reliability and performance

  • Technical debt reduction

  • Architecture improvements

Team Impact

  • Knowledge sharing

  • Mentorship

  • Process improvements

  • Team productivity

  • Collaboration effectiveness

Business Impact

  • Customer satisfaction

  • Product success

  • Revenue influence

  • Cost optimization

  • Market competitiveness

Impact Across Levels

Individual Contributors

  • IC1: Impact through individual contributions and learning

  • IC2: Impact through project leadership and mentoring

  • IC3: Impact through technical leadership within team

  • IC4: Impact through cross-team technical leadership

  • IC5: Impact through department technical leadership

  • IC6: Impact through company-wide technical leadership

  • IC7: Impact through senior company-wide technical leadership

Engineering Managers

  • M3: Impact through team success and individual growth

  • M4: Impact through multiple team effectiveness

  • M5: Impact through department success

Growing Your Impact

Key Strategies

  • Seek feedback regularly

  • Take on challenging projects

  • Learn from failures

  • Share knowledge

  • Build relationships

  • Think long-term

Common Pitfalls

  • Focusing on activity over outcomes

  • Not considering business context

  • Working in isolation

  • Avoiding difficult problems

  • Neglecting sustainability

Measuring Success

Quantitative Metrics

  • Project completion rates

  • System performance metrics

  • Quality metrics

  • Team velocity

  • Customer satisfaction scores

Qualitative Indicators

  • Peer feedback

  • Code quality

  • Documentation quality

  • Knowledge sharing

  • Team collaboration

Remember that impact is not just about individual achievements—it’s about creating lasting positive change that helps TTech and our customers succeed.
