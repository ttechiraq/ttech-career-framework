Site Reliability Engineer

Overview

Site Reliability Engineers at TTech are responsible for ensuring the reliability, scalability, and performance of our software systems. They work at the intersection of software development and operations, applying software engineering principles to solve operational problems.

Career Levels

Level Scope Impact Key Responsibilities

IC1

Individual services and components

Team-level impact

  • Learning SRE practices and tools

  • Working under guidance

  • Contributing to system reliability

  • Monitoring and alerting

IC2

Multiple services and systems

Cross-team impact

  • Working independently

  • Implementing reliability improvements

  • Mentoring junior SREs

  • Leading incident response

IC3

Complex distributed systems

Organization-wide impact

  • Technical leadership

  • System architecture and reliability

  • Mentoring senior SREs

  • Driving reliability standards

IC1 Site Reliability Engineer

IC1 Site Reliability Engineer is the entry-level position for SREs. They focus on learning SRE practices and contributing to system reliability under guidance.

IC2 Site Reliability Engineer

IC2 Site Reliability Engineer is the mid-level position for SREs. They work independently on reliability improvements and lead incident response.

IC3 Site Reliability Engineer

IC3 Site Reliability Engineer is the senior-level position for SREs. They are technical leaders who drive system reliability and architecture.
