TTech Career Framework

Overview

The TTech Career Framework is your source for understanding how to achieve impact in your role and grow in your engineering career. For managers, it helps set expectations with teams and hold them accountable for their work.

Since impact is our primary measure of success, we recommend starting with What is Impact? to understand how we think about and measure impact at TTech.

What the Career Framework is not

This framework is not a promotion checklist for your role; rather, it’s designed to help you understand what impact could look like at the next level.

This framework is not an exhaustive list of examples and behaviors; each responsibility includes key behaviors that serve as a guide for how to think about your work. You’ll need to meet with your manager to define your impact goals and align on the expectations for your role.

What’s in the Career Framework?

This framework is broken down into two components:

  • Level Expectations define the scope, collaborative reach, and levers for impact at every level

  • Core and Craft Responsibilities define the key behaviors specific to your role and team

How to navigate this framework

TTech measures the success of its engineers largely on business impact. Anchor your work first and foremost on creating long-term impact.

Next, ground yourself in the expectations for your level and team. For each level, you’ll find a summary description and the role’s scope, collaborative reach, and levers for impact.

Review the expected behaviors for that level across the Results, Direction, Talent, and Culture pillars from the Core Responsibilities. Read your Craft expectations, which are the per-discipline technical capabilities you need to master at that level. Finally, meet with your manager to set your goals for the quarter.

Core Responsibilities

Pillar Description

Results

Delivering business value and impact

Direction

Setting and executing strategy

Talent

Developing and growing people

Culture

Fostering a positive work environment

Ownership

Taking full responsibility for project success and team outcomes

Ownership and Accountability

Aspect Description

Project Ownership

Taking full responsibility for project success, including quality, timelines, and business impact

Team Success

Actively contributing to team goals and supporting colleagues

Problem Resolution

Proactively identifying and addressing issues before they impact the business

Continuous Improvement

Taking initiative to improve processes, code quality, and team effectiveness

Stakeholder Communication

Keeping stakeholders informed and managing expectations proactively
